## 🧭 Task 1: Introduction to SOC

As organizations increasingly rely on technology, they also become more dependent on the security of their digital assets. Sensitive information—including customer data, financial records, intellectual property, and credentials—is now primarily stored on networks and systems rather than in physical files.

Threat actors continuously search for vulnerabilities to exploit, making cybersecurity a critical business function. Traditional security measures alone are often insufficient to defend against modern threats.

To address this challenge, organizations establish a **Security Operations Center (SOC)**.

A **SOC (Security Operations Center)** is a centralized team of security professionals responsible for continuously monitoring, detecting, investigating, and responding to cybersecurity threats across an organization's environment.

Most SOC teams operate:

```text
24 hours a day
7 days a week
365 days a year
```

Their primary objective is to quickly identify suspicious activity and minimize potential damage.

## 🧭 Task 2: Purpose and Components

The primary mission of every SOC is built around two core capabilities:

* Detection
* Response

To achieve this, SOC teams rely on centralized security tools that continuously monitor systems, endpoints, users, and network activity.

### 🔍 Detection

Detection focuses on identifying security issues before they become major incidents.

#### Detect Vulnerabilities

A **vulnerability** is a weakness that attackers may exploit.

Examples include:

* Unpatched operating systems
* Outdated software
* Misconfigured services

Although patch management is often handled by another team, the SOC monitors vulnerabilities because they directly affect the organization's security posture.

#### Detect Unauthorized Activity

The SOC identifies activities performed without proper authorization.

Example:

```text
An attacker logs into an employee account using stolen credentials.
```

Indicators may include:

* Unusual login locations
* Impossible travel
* Suspicious login times

#### Detect Policy Violations

Organizations establish security policies to protect their assets.

Examples of violations include:

* Downloading pirated software
* Sharing confidential files insecurely
* Installing unauthorized applications

#### Detect Intrusions

An **intrusion** occurs when an attacker gains unauthorized access to systems or networks.

Examples:

* Exploiting a vulnerable web application
* Malware infection
* Remote compromise

### 🚨 Response

Detection alone is not enough.

Once suspicious activity is confirmed, the SOC supports the incident response process.

Responsibilities include:

* Containment
* Investigation
* Root cause analysis
* Recovery support
* Documentation

### 🏛️ The Three Pillars of SOC

Every mature SOC is built upon three essential pillars:

1. People
2. Process
3. Technology

These three components work together to provide effective security operations.

## 🧭 Task 3: People

Technology alone cannot secure an organization.

Security tools generate thousands of alerts every day, many of which are false positives.

Human analysts determine:

* Which alerts are legitimate
* Which require escalation
* Which can be safely dismissed

The SOC team consists of several specialized roles.

### 👨‍💻 SOC Analyst (Level 1)

Level 1 Analysts are the first responders.

Responsibilities include:

* Monitoring alerts
* Performing initial alert triage
* Validating detections
* Escalating genuine incidents
* Reporting findings

### 🔎 SOC Analyst (Level 2)

Level 2 Analysts perform deeper investigations.

Responsibilities include:

* Threat analysis
* Log correlation
* Advanced investigations
* Supporting Level 1 Analysts

### 🎯 SOC Analyst (Level 3)

Level 3 Analysts are senior security professionals.

Responsibilities include:

* Threat hunting
* Incident response
* Malware analysis
* Containment
* Recovery support

### ⚙️ Security Engineer

Security Engineers deploy and maintain security infrastructure.

Responsibilities include:

* SIEM deployment
* EDR configuration
* Firewall management
* System maintenance

### 🛠️ Detection Engineer

Detection Engineers develop detection logic.

Responsibilities include:

* Writing detection rules
* Improving alert quality
* Reducing false positives
* Maintaining detection coverage

### 👔 SOC Manager

The SOC Manager oversees daily operations.

Responsibilities include:

* Team management
* Process improvement
* Reporting security posture
* Coordinating with executive leadership

### ✅ Answers

#### Alert triage and reporting are primarily handled by?

```text
SOC Analyst (Level 1)
```

#### Which role is responsible for creating security detection rules?

```text
Detection Engineer
```

## 🧭 Task 4: Process

Processes define how the SOC responds consistently to security events.

### 🚦 Alert Triage

Alert triage is the first step after receiving a security alert.

Its purpose is to:

* Validate the alert
* Determine severity
* Prioritize response

SOC analysts typically answer the **5 Ws**.

### ❓ The 5 Ws

Example Alert:

```text
Malware detected on GEORGE-PC
```

| W | Example |
|---|---------|
| What | Malware detected |
| When | June 5, 2024 – 13:20 |
| Where | GEORGE-PC |
| Who | George |
| Why | Downloaded pirated software |

The 5 Ws provide enough context for escalation and investigation.

### 📝 Reporting

Confirmed security events are documented and escalated.

A good report should include:

* The 5 Ws
* Investigation findings
* Supporting evidence
* Screenshots
* Recommended actions

Reports are usually converted into incident tickets.

### 🔬 Incident Response & Forensics

Critical alerts often require a formal incident response process.

Typical activities include:

* Containment
* Eradication
* Recovery
* Lessons learned

Sometimes forensic investigations are also performed to determine:

* Root cause
* Timeline
* Attacker actions

### ✅ Answers

#### The investigation determined John attempted to steal company data. Which W is this?

```text
Who
```

#### The SOC detected large-scale data exfiltration. Which W is this?

```text
What
```

## 🧭 Task 5: Technology

Technology enables the SOC to monitor large environments efficiently.

Instead of manually reviewing every device, security tools centralize visibility and automate detection.

### 📊 SIEM

**Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)**

A SIEM collects logs from multiple sources, correlates events, and generates alerts based on detection rules.

Modern SIEM platforms also provide:

* User Behavior Analytics (UBA)
* Threat Intelligence
* Machine Learning
* Correlation Rules

**Primary Function**

```text
Detection
```

### 💻 EDR

**Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)**

EDR monitors endpoints such as:

* Workstations
* Laptops
* Servers

Capabilities include:

* Real-time monitoring
* Historical investigations
* Malware detection
* Automated response
* Endpoint isolation

### 🔥 Firewall

A firewall controls network traffic entering and leaving an organization's network.

Responsibilities include:

* Traffic filtering
* Blocking unauthorized connections
* Network segmentation
* Detecting suspicious traffic

### 🛡️ Other Common SOC Technologies

Additional tools often deployed include:

* Antivirus
* EPP (Endpoint Protection Platform)
* IDS (Intrusion Detection System)
* IPS (Intrusion Prevention System)
* XDR (Extended Detection and Response)
* SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation and Response)

Organizations choose technologies based on:

* Risk
* Budget
* Infrastructure
* Threat landscape

## 📝 Key Takeaways

* A SOC continuously monitors and protects an organization's infrastructure.
* The two primary SOC objectives are Detection and Response.
* Every mature SOC is built on the three pillars:
  * People
  * Process
  * Technology
* Level 1 Analysts perform alert triage and escalation.
* Level 2 Analysts conduct deeper investigations.
* Level 3 Analysts perform threat hunting and incident response.
* Detection Engineers create and improve detection rules.
* Security Engineers deploy and maintain security tools.
* The 5 Ws help analysts investigate alerts effectively.
* SIEM solutions primarily provide centralized detection and alerting.
* EDR provides endpoint visibility and response capabilities.
* Firewalls protect networks by filtering inbound and outbound traffic.
* Documentation and reporting are essential components of incident response.
